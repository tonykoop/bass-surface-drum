## Strike Map (V0)

The bass-surface-drum has effectively **2 strike zones**:

- **Center** — pure (0,1) fundamental at ~50 Hz. Sustained, room-filling.
- **Off-center (1/3 radius from rim)** — two-tone transient with (1,1) at ~80 Hz.

Plus a third optional zone:

- **Rim** — high overtone content; useful for accents and stops.

## Suggested Beginner Exercises

### Exercise 1 — Sustained Pulse

- Beat: half-note = 60 BPM
- Pattern: center strike on every downbeat, let ring full
- Goal: develop a feel for the sustained boom and how it bridges between strikes
- 5 minutes/day for one week

### Exercise 2 — Center / Off-Center Conversation

- Beat: quarter-note = 80 BPM
- Pattern: `1 — center, 2 — center, 3 — off-center, 4 — center` (in 4/4)
- Goal: hear the (0,1)-only vs. (0,1)+(1,1) timbre difference
- 5 minutes/day for one week

### Exercise 3 — Cinematic Build

- Beat: half-note = 50 BPM (slow)
- Pattern: 4 measures center strike each bar, then 4 measures center+off-center alternating, then 4 measures all center with quarter-note roll on bar 4
- Goal: experience the dramatic build a single drum can carry
- One pass per practice session
