# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished, very large single-head frame drum standing as a real physical object: a rolled cold-rolled-steel cylinder roughly the diameter of a tabletop, shallow in depth, painted matte black, with a single warm tan natural-hide head stretched tight across the top. The recognizable signature is the all-metal frame — a smooth outward-curled bearing flange where the hide rolls over the top rim, a separate matte-black steel clamp ring pressing the head down, and six evenly spaced M10 tension bolts and brackets around the circumference holding the ring against the frame. A subtle convex stiffening bead rolls around the lower edge of the cylinder. The hide shows fine fiber direction and natural mottled brown-to-tan tone; the steel reads as low-sheen industrial paint with faint gold catching only on the rolled edges. Scale is unmistakably massive — this is an instrument you strike standing or seated beside it. Clean neutral cream-paper studio background, soft directional studio light from upper-left with cooler fill from the right, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The bass surface drum staged in a maker's workshop or on a dim concert stage, resting low on a simple stand or floor cradle, with a person seated beside it holding one soft felt-headed cinematic mallet poised over the center of the hide — the human body next to the huge drum makes the very-large scale instantly legible. Warm natural side-light rakes across the tan hide to show its fiber and the matte-black steel frame, brackets and clamp ring catching faint highlights. Shallow depth of field, soft background falloff, environmental storytelling of a deep-boom processional instrument, concept visualization only, photorealistic.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the signature edge sandwich: the warm tan natural hide rolling over the smooth outward-curled steel bearing flange at the top rim, the matte-black steel clamp ring pressing it down, and one M10 bolt with washer drawing the ring against a welded bracket on the outside of the black cylinder. Raking low-angle light grazes the curl of the flange and the convex stiffening bead lower on the frame, revealing the difference between the soft fibrous hide texture and the hard low-sheen painted steel, with faint gold glints only on the rolled edges. High detail, shallow focus, no measurements, no callouts.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension lines, measurement callouts, or tuning numbers; no impossible mechanisms or extra bolts beyond a plausible even ring; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
