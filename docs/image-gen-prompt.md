# Bass Surface Drum Image-Gen Prompts

Concept visualization only; not fabrication authority. Do not infer final frame
geometry, lug layout, head tension, or tuning from these prompts.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a very large single-head bass surface drum: an 800 mm class circular natural-skin head stretched over a rolled cold-rolled steel frame, with a smooth outward bearing flange at the top, a rolled stiffening bead near the bottom, and a practical clamp-ring tension system with a small number of robust external bolts. The object should read as a cinematic sub-bass frame drum, broad and shallow, with a soft oversized bass mallet placed nearby for scale. Clean studio background, muted steel finish, natural hide texture, soft directional lighting, 50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic scoring-stage or workshop scene of the bass surface drum on a low stand, being played with a large soft mallet near the center of the head for a deep boom. Include subtle measurement and fabrication context: a drum dial, torque wrench, rolled steel offcut, and a quiet room with acoustic panels or shop walls. Natural light or soft studio light, believable seated or standing player posture, huge scale without exaggeration.

## Detail / Macro Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the bass surface drum rim: thick natural hide folded under a clamp ring, smooth rolled steel bearing radius, a tension bolt and bracket, and the subtle texture of dressed metal near a TIG seam. Show the hide fibers, steel finish, and bolt hardware clearly, with no measurement labels. High-detail macro, shallow depth of field.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, tiny toy scale, impossible lug geometry, torn hide, extra limbs, distorted mallets, or fake pitch labels.
